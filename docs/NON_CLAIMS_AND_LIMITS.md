# Non-Claims and Limits

This document defines what IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid does not claim.

These limits are part of the project’s credibility. They are not optional disclaimers added after the fact. They are core design boundaries.

## Core Non-Claim

IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid does not claim to make any city, district, barangay, road, building, evacuation center, or drainage basin flood-proof.

The project is a reference architecture for targeted flood-resilience support, not a guarantee of flood prevention.

## Not a Certified Product

This repository is not a certified flood-control product.

It is not certified by any government agency, standards body, engineering board, disaster-risk authority, public-works department, utility, or emergency-management office.

No file in this repository should be interpreted as a product certification, deployment approval, safety approval, procurement approval, or construction authorization.

## Not Construction-Ready

This repository is not a construction-ready design.

It does not include stamped engineering drawings, final construction specifications, site-specific hydraulic calculations, geotechnical analysis, electrical permitting documents, environmental permits, or approved public-works plans.

Any real deployment requires qualified professional review and local approval.

## Not a Replacement for Licensed Engineering

IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid does not replace licensed civil, hydraulic, structural, electrical, environmental, mechanical, fire-safety, or public-works engineering.

The repository can support early evaluation, pilot scoping, and proof-of-concept planning. It cannot substitute for site-specific engineering.

## Not a Replacement for Drainage Infrastructure

The system does not replace:

- storm drains;
- canals;
- culverts;
- pumping stations;
- flood gates;
- retention basins;
- river-basin management;
- coastal defenses;
- solid-waste management;
- public-works maintenance;
- emergency evacuation planning.

It is intended to support selected chokepoints and improve visibility, pumping support, power resilience, and maintenance response.

## Not a Typhoon Stopper

The system does not stop typhoons, monsoon rainfall, storm surge, river overflow, sea-level rise, or basin-scale flooding.

It cannot prevent rainfall from occurring and cannot overcome floodwater volumes that exceed the available drainage, pumping, storage, or discharge capacity.

## Not a Citywide Miracle Solution

A HydroGrid Lot can only affect the area connected to its intake, pump, sensor, power, discharge, and maintenance systems.

A network of HydroGrid Lots may support a larger district, but it still cannot eliminate citywide flooding unless paired with adequate drainage infrastructure, receiving water capacity, maintenance, and public-works integration.

## Not Useful Without a Discharge Path

Water must go somewhere.

A pump-supported flood system requires a valid discharge route, such as:

- existing storm drain;
- canal;
- culvert;
- retention basin;
- outfall;
- pump station;
- swale;
- temporary storage;
- approved transfer path.

If there is no legal and hydraulically valid discharge path, pumping may simply move water from one problem area to another.

No HydroGrid deployment should be approved without discharge-path review.

## Not Useful Without Maintenance

Debris can disable flood infrastructure.

HydroGrid systems require maintenance ownership, including:

- clearing debris baskets;
- inspecting screens;
- checking pumps;
- replacing worn components;
- cleaning sensors;
- verifying batteries;
- reviewing logs;
- testing communications;
- inspecting discharge lines;
- correcting vandalism or storm damage.

If maintenance is not assigned, funded, and scheduled, the system should be considered at high risk of failure.

## Not Validated Without Measurement

No deployment should be described as validated unless it has measured evidence.

Required evidence may include:

- water-level measurements;
- calibrated flow measurements;
- pump runtime logs;
- power consumption logs;
- rainfall data;
- clog detection events;
- maintenance records;
- discharge observations;
- controlled wet-test results;
- approved field-pilot data.

Estimated performance is not validation.

A design target is not validation.

A simulated example is not validation.

A concept diagram is not validation.

## No Fake Field Data

This repository must not contain fake field results, fake Manila deployment logs, fake government interest, fake partner claims, fake pump results, fake sensor readings, or fictional flood-event outcomes.

Templates and schemas may be included for future real data collection.

Hypothetical examples may be included only if clearly labeled as hypothetical or illustrative and never presented as measured results.

## No Guaranteed Life Safety

IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid does not guarantee life safety.

Floods can create fast-moving water, electrical hazards, contaminated water, open manholes, debris impacts, structural damage, vehicle hazards, and evacuation constraints.

Any life-safety use requires integration with official emergency-management procedures, evacuation planning, public warnings, and qualified safety review.

## No Guaranteed Property Protection

The system does not guarantee protection of homes, businesses, vehicles, roads, public buildings, utilities, or infrastructure.

It may reduce flood duration or improve local response at selected locations if properly designed, sited, installed, powered, maintained, and validated.

## No Hydrogen Floodwater Removal Claim

IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid does not claim that electrolysis or hydrogen production removes floodwater at useful flood-control scale.

Hydrogen, if included in future configurations, is treated only as a possible backup-energy pathway for powering pumps, sensors, communications, lights, or emergency support equipment.

Hydrogen systems require certified equipment, code-compliant storage, ventilation, fire-safety review, professional installation, and local approval.

Hydrogen is not used as a magical water-disposal mechanism.

## No Raw Floodwater Electrolysis Claim

The project does not recommend uncontrolled electrolysis of raw floodwater.

Floodwater may contain salt, sewage, hydrocarbons, metals, sediment, organic matter, biological contamination, and other unknown pollutants.

Electrochemical systems using contaminated or chloride-bearing water can create serious safety, corrosion, gas-quality, and toxic byproduct risks.

Any electrochemical water treatment or hydrogen system requires proper pretreatment, monitoring, safety controls, and professional review.

## No Defense, Weapon, or Disabling Function

IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid is a civilian flood-resilience project.

It does not include:

- targeting behavior;
- weapon behavior;
- electronic attack;
- EMP effects;
- disabling logic;
- stealth behavior;
- sonar disruption;
- hostile autonomy;
- military engagement logic.

Safe concepts from unrelated donor material may only be reused as civilian water-sensing, ruggedization, power-buffering, event-logging, and flood-classification ideas.

## No Surveillance Claim

The system is not intended for personal surveillance.

Sensors should be focused on environmental and infrastructure conditions, including water level, flow, rainfall, pump operation, clogging, salinity, pressure, turbidity, vibration, and equipment health.

If cameras are used, privacy review and local policy must apply.

## No Universal Site Claim

The system will not work equally well everywhere.

Performance depends on:

- rainfall intensity;
- flood source;
- site elevation;
- drainage network;
- receiving water level;
- discharge capacity;
- debris load;
- power availability;
- communications availability;
- maintenance access;
- vandalism risk;
- regulatory approval;
- crew response time.

Each site must be evaluated independently.

## No Cost Certainty

This repository may include bill-of-materials tiers and component classes, but it does not guarantee final cost.

Real costs depend on:

- country and city;
- supplier availability;
- import duties;
- labor;
- permits;
- engineering fees;
- civil works;
- pumping capacity;
- battery size;
- communications plan;
- maintenance;
- site preparation;
- safety requirements;
- long-term operations.

Any cost estimate must be treated as preliminary until verified by local procurement and engineering review.

## No Procurement Approval

This repository does not approve any vendor, product, supplier, contractor, or procurement route.

Component categories are provided for planning. Final procurement must follow local law, agency rules, engineering requirements, safety standards, and site-specific design.

## No Environmental Approval

The system may interact with stormwater, floodwater, canals, drains, outfalls, sediments, debris, or contaminated water.

Any deployment requires environmental review where applicable.

No file in this repository authorizes discharge, diversion, treatment, storage, or pumping of water without local approval.

## No Public-Works Override

The system must not be used to bypass public-works authority.

Any deployment in public space, drainage systems, roads, canals, pump stations, or waterways requires coordination with the responsible public agencies and infrastructure owners.

## No Emergency Authority

This repository does not grant emergency authority, site access, public-road access, utility access, or installation rights.

All field activity must be approved by relevant property owners, agencies, and authorities.

## What the System Can Reasonably Claim

With proper design, siting, review, maintenance, and validation, IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid may reasonably aim to support:

- earlier detection of rising water;
- better visibility into flood chokepoints;
- measured pump support at selected sites;
- debris-aware maintenance prioritization;
- detection of intake clogging;
- detection of outlet blockage;
- detection of backflow conditions;
- local backup power for sensors, pumps, lights, and communications;
- event logging for post-storm review;
- faster maintenance response;
- reduced flood duration at selected locations;
- improved access preservation for priority routes where hydraulically feasible.

These are bounded, testable goals.

## Evidence Standard

A claim is acceptable only if it is tied to one of the following:

- known engineering principle;
- cited technical reference;
- clearly marked design assumption;
- measurable acceptance target;
- controlled test result;
- real field data from an approved deployment;
- documented maintenance record;
- verified sensor log.

Unverified performance language must be avoided.

## Summary

IX-Bayanihan-HydroGrid is intentionally bounded.

It is not a miracle flood solution. It is a disciplined reference architecture for testing whether modular sensing, pump support, backup power, debris-aware maintenance, and event logging can reduce specific flood pain points at selected sites.

The project should be judged by measured performance, not by promises.
